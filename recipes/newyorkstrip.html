The New York Strip

A wonderfully cooked New York strip steak

The New York Strip is a cult classic amongst steak enthusiast and casual steak enjoyers alike. It is derived from the short loin, making it quite tender.

Ingredients
  • 12 oz short loin new york strip steak
  • 1/2 tbsp favorite steak seasoning
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 8 springs fresh thyme
  • 3 cloves garlic
    • Steps
    1. Allow your steak to come to room temperature.
    2. Prepare your favorite steak seasoning.
    3. Heat up your skillet on medium heat with olive oil until smoke is just beggining to rise.
    4. Season your steak, being sure to pat it in and not rub. This shouldn't be done any more than 3 minutes before cooking.
    5. Place your steak, garlic, and herbs into the hot skillet and allow it to cook for 3 minutes, unmoved. Then flip the steak and allow it another 3 minutes.
    6. Flip again, add butter, and let cook for two minutes. After that two minutes, arroser the butter onto the steak.
    7. Remove steak after it reaches an internal temperature of 135 degrees.
    8. Loosely tent your steak with aluminium foil and let sit for at least 5 minutes.
